Supermarkets

Undoubtedly, the best place to get ingredients is the supermarket. This place provides afforable ingredients to test out your cooking

RedMan

Over the years, there are certain places that have also become my go-to places for ingredients. One such place is RedMan by Phoon Huat. It sells ingredients that are hard to find elsewhere, such as sodium alginate which are used for molecular gastronomy. Molecular gastronomy is a branch of food science that focuses on the physical and chemical processes that arise when cooking. The picture on the right shows various molecular gastronomy dishes made during the Everyday Science in Foods Module taken in NIE. One of which is the egg, which was made from milk and mango puree.

The Cheese Shop

During my attempts at carbonara, I scoured for different types of cheeses. One such store that I frequently visited was The Cheese Shop. The Cheese Shop provided a wide variety of cheese that is hard to find elsewhere.
